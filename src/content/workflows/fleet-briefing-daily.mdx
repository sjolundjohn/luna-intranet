---
name: "Daily fleet briefing"
summary: "Generates a short written report and a podcast-format audio episode of what the fleet has been working on in the last 24 hours."
owner: "john"
trigger: "scheduled"
schedule: "0 18 * * *"
status: "drafting"
order: 2
steps:
  - name: "Pull yesterday's audit log"
    kind: "fetch"
    summary: "All agent invocations grouped by agent and team. Aggregate-only — no individual employee names."
  - name: "Pull workflow run summaries"
    kind: "fetch"
    summary: "Workflow runs that completed, failed, or are in flight. Step-level timing and cost."
  - name: "Per-team summarizers (parallel)"
    kind: "agent"
    agent: "basal"
    summary: "Fan out via Queues — one summarizer per team, each writes a 3-bullet digest of its team's AI activity."
  - name: "Compose master briefing"
    kind: "agent"
    agent: "basal"
    summary: "Stitch the per-team digests into a single executive briefing. Sections: shipped, hot, anomalies, coming next."
  - name: "Render to TTS"
    kind: "transform"
    summary: "Phase 4: synthesize the briefing as MP3 audio for the podcast feed. Stored in R2."
  - name: "Publish"
    kind: "deliver"
    summary: "Write episode row to D1; static page reads latest. RSS feed regenerates."
---

The daily fleet briefing is the single most visible Cloudflare Workflow on the platform. It's the surface that makes the AI fleet's work legible to leadership and the team, in a form that respects their time — read it in 90 seconds or listen while making coffee.

The privacy discipline is built into the workflow: aggregation happens at the per-team level, individual employee names never appear in the output, and prompt content is never quoted. The briefing reports on the work, not on who did it.

The workflow lives in `apps/workflow-runner/` once Phase 3 lands; today it's an MDX-defined shape we read into the catalog page.
