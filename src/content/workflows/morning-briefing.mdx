---
name: "Morning briefing"
summary: "Per-person summary of inbox, calendar, and pending decisions, delivered before standup."
owner: "john"
trigger: "scheduled"
schedule: "0 7 * * 1-5"
status: "active"
order: 1
steps:
  - name: "Pull mail since yesterday"
    kind: "fetch"
    summary: "Read the user's Gmail since 6pm prior business day, filter signal vs newsletter."
  - name: "Pull today's calendar"
    kind: "fetch"
    summary: "Today's meetings + any prep docs linked from invites."
  - name: "Pull pending decisions"
    kind: "fetch"
    summary: "Open PRs, open Notion docs assigned to user, pending HubSpot approvals."
  - name: "Compose briefing"
    kind: "agent"
    agent: "basal"
    summary: "Two paragraphs + a 3-item action list. Keep under 250 words."
  - name: "Deliver to Slack DM"
    kind: "deliver"
    summary: "Post the briefing as a Slack DM from Basal at 7:00 local time."
---

The morning briefing is the canonical example of the workflow shape we're optimizing for: a scheduled multi-step task that compresses a long thread of context into a structured starting point for the day.

It's a Class B (personal) workflow — each person gets their own version with their own sources. The schedule is 7am local time on weekdays, delivered to a Slack DM from Basal. The output is intentionally short: two paragraphs and three actions, designed to fit in a glance before standup.

The workflow's success metric is "did the user open Slack and immediately know what they were going to do today?" — which we measure indirectly through whether they trigger any of the actions in the first hour.
